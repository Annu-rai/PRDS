16 Days Activisam

A 16 days activism campaign is being celebrated all over the world from 25th November to 10th December every year.

It is a day to educate on the issue of “No violence against women and child”

This 16-days activism is the programme being organized internationally in order to fight the violence against women and children in the world to create awareness in the people on gender discrimination. A white ribbon was tied to arms of the people who assembled during these events.

Rangoli

Sankranthi is the festival of Harvest – i.e., the crops are harvested and the produce is brought to home. Traditionally, on the eve of Sankranthi festival, the women draw Rangolis in front of their houses, the Haridas visit the households and collect food grains, several activities are organized in the villages.

As a part of promoting the tradition of Rangoli, competitions are a part of Organisation’s activities every year. PRDS takes up these competitions in the month of January, when, the festival of Sankranthi is celebrated in the State of Andhra Pradesh.

The Rangoli competitions, organized by PRDS, help the women in promoting mental peace as they get a chance to come out, compete with other women, in a friendly and spirited atmosphere. These competitions bring out the skills and talents among the rural women and encourage them for sharpening their skills. These are open competitions with no age limit. Rangoli competitions brought the festival atmosphere.

The winners of these competitions are awarded First, Second and Third prizes as a part of encouraging their spirit. are to be allotted.

Legal Awareness Camps

PRDS has a special focus to support the women who are in the clutches of violence of different types. In addition to this it is providing economical service to the poor women who are in severe distress.

In order to provide awareness to the women about their rights and rules, legal awareness camps are conducted in the villages, where the women are explained about Women Rights and responsibilities with suitable examples. Support of technically qualified persons like Ms. Balasundari, Advocate, local police officials, women activities are taken during these camps. Though Domestic Violence Act is under prevalence women are not keeping their foot forward to lodge complaints against their husbands or on in–laws or any other family members, for fear of social and traditional reasons. About 1080 women have attended these meetings organized between 2010 -2013.
